The 2019–20 coronavirus pandemic spread to South Africa, with the first case confirmed on 5 March 2020 by Minister of Health Zweli Mkhize.[2] As of 20 March 2020[update], there were a total of 202 confirmed cases with 0 deaths and 2 recoveries. [1]

On 15 March, the first internal transmission was confirmed by President Cyril Ramaphosa, who declared a national state of disaster lasting for three months if not extended or cancelled, with a partial travel ban, travel advisories, discouraging public transport, the closing of schools, and prohibiting gatherings of more than 100 people.[3]

Preparations[edit]

After the World Health Organisation (WHO) declared COVID-19 a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 30 January 2020, an emergency operation centre was activated.[4]

On 1 March 2020, the first patient later confirmed with COVID-19 in South Africa, returned with his wife and 8 others from the province of Milan in Italy,[5] travelling via Dubai, O. R. Tambo International Airport in Kempton Park, Gauteng[6] and King Shaka International Airport in Durban to Hilton.[7] On 3 March, the patient reported with symptoms to a private general practitioner and isolated himself; the doctor isolated herself as well. On 5 March Mkhize announced the first confirmed case, epidemiologists and clinicians from the National Institute for Communicable Diseases (NICD) were deployed to KwaZulu-Natal in response,[2][8][9] and the patient went to Grey's Hospital in Pietermaritzburg.[10]

On 7 March, it was announced that a woman from the same travel group from Italy, returning to Gauteng, also tested positive.[11]

On 11 March, 6 new cases were reported, with 1 case from the same travel group from Italy, while the other 5 cases appear unrelated having travel histories to other European countries. The first case was confirmed in the Western Cape province.[12]

On 12 March, 3 new cases were announced, including the first case in Mpumalanga province. The first local transmission and first case in the Free State province was also announced, but withdrawn later in the day by the NICD who confirmed that the case was in fact negative.[13] This brought the total cases to 16.[14][15][16]

On 15 March, the first local transmissions, not yet confirmed by government labs, was announced by President Cyril Ramaphosa.[3]

On 16 March, the first confirmed case from Limpopo province was announced.[17]

On 17 March, the first confirmed cases of local transmission were announced by government labs, 4 in Gauteng, 3 in KwaZulu-Natal, and 1 in the Western Cape.[18]

On 18 March, the first confirmed case of local transmission in Mpumalanga was announced by government labs.[19]

On 19 March, the confirmed number of cases climbed to 150.[20][21] Later that evening, Mkhize suggested that two thirds of the South African population could contract the virus. This prediction falls in line with Europe's estimates on population infection.[22]

Impact[edit]

Repatriation[edit]

On 14 March, 114 South Africans repatriated from Wuhan, China land in Polokwane.[51]

Gatherings limited[edit]

On 15 March, gatherings of more than 100 people were prohibited.[3]

On 18 March, Minister of Cooperative Governance and Traditional Affairs Nkosazana Dlamini-Zuma signed a government gazette limiting the number of patrons at pubs, clubs, and restaurants to 50.[52]

School closures[edit]

All schools will be closed from 18 March 2020 until after the Easter season,[3] resuming on 14 April, with the June holidays shortened by a week.[53]

Events cancelled or postponed[edit]

Government and politics

  • Parliament suspended all activities as of 18 March.[60]
  • The African National Congress and Democratic Alliance postponed their elective conferences.[61]

Sport

  • Super Rugby tournament, all matches suspended.[62]
  • 2019-20 Pro14 season, suspended. [63]
  • Varsity Rugby, suspended.[64]
  • Two Oceans Marathon, cancelled.[65] [66]
  • Cape Epic, cancelled. [67]
  • Sunshine Tour, all golf events suspended.[68]
  • PSL, all football fixtures suspended.[69]
  • Athletics South Africa, all athletics events postponed.[70]
  • Parkrun, all athletics events postponed.[71]
  • ATP and WTA Tennis Tours, suspended until 7 June.[72]

Live entertainment

  • Bloem Show, cancelled.[73]
  • AfrikaBurn, cancelled.[74]
  • Cape Town International Jazz Festival, postponed.[75][76]
  • Joyous Celebration 24 Launch, at Carnival City cancelled
  • Klein Karoo Nasionale Kunstefees (KKNK), cancelled.[77]
  • Lekkerland Carnival, postponed.
  • Lighthouse Family, South African tour postponed.[78]
  • Boys II Men, South African tour postponed.[79]
  • BeBe Winans, South African tour postponed.[80]
  • Splashy Fen Festival, rescheduled.[81]
  • Rand Show 2020, postponed.[82]

Religious

  • Church activities of the Dutch Reformed Church, suspended and moved to WhatsApp groups.[citation needed]
  • Zion Christian Church (ZCC) Easter pilgrimage, cancelled until further notice.[83]
  • Muslim Judicial Council (MJC) suspends Friday prayers but keeps mosques open.[84]
  • Chief Rabbi Warren Goldstein suspends Synagogues.[85]

Other

  • University of Pretoria, University of Cape Town, Stellenbosch University, Rhodes University, University of KwaZulu Natal and Durban University of Technology graduation ceremonies cancelled or postponed until further notice.[86][87][88][89]
  • Comic Con Cape Town, postponed to 2021.[90]
  • Agricultural events such as the Pietermaritzburg Royal Show, SA Cheese Festival, Qualité Awards Dinner, and Agri-Expo Western Cape Youth Show, cancelled.[91]
  • Wimpy Lifesaving South Africa, national championships cancelled.[92]
  • Scouts South Africa, all Scouting activity suspended until schools reopen.[93]
  • The City of Johannesburg, all public facilities closed indefinitely including public swimming pools, recreational and civic centres, stadiums, libraries, sporting facilities, and the Johannesburg Zoo.[94]

Travel[edit]

President Cyril Ramaphosa announced that from 18 March 2020, a travel ban will be imposed on foreign nationals travelling from high-risk countries and that visas for travellers from those countries have been cancelled.[3]

Border post closures[edit]

From 16 March, 35 of 53 land ports will be shut down. 2 of the 8 sea ports will be closed[3] for passengers and crew changes: Mossel Bay and Saldanha.[96] On 19 March, it was announced by Minister of Public Works and Infrastructure Patricia de Lille that the government would erect and repair 40km of fencing to the east and west of the Beitbridge border post between South Africa and Zimbabwe.[97]

Stock market news[edit]

The JSE lost 15% of its value in the week ending 13 March 2020, its worst week in 21 years.[98]

On 19 March, the South African Reserve Bank governor, Lesetja Kganyago announced a reduction of the country's repo rate by 100 basis points or 1% to 5.25%.[99]

Retail[edit]

Retailers like Clicks, Pick n Pay, and Woolworths have some limitations on the number of the same items customers may buy.[100] On 19 March, Minister of Trade and Industry Ebrahim Patel signed a government gazette that enforces price controls on essential items and that could see price gougers punished with measures including a R10 million fine, a fine equivalent to 10% of a firm's turnover, or 12 months in prison.[101]
